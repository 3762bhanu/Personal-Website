#### Introduction

My name is Bhanu Prakash Singh. I am a software engineer with a focus on backend systems, distributed architecture, and platform engineering. I currently work in the retail-tech space building high-throughput services that power product valuation and pricing workflows.

I enjoy working close to the foundations of systems — understanding how things actually work rather than just using abstractions that make them easier to use.

#### Work

My day-to-day involves designing and building microservices in Java and Python, working with Kubernetes for deployment and operations, and thinking about system reliability and performance at scale.

I have a strong interest in data-intensive applications, event-driven architectures, and the operational side of running software in production. I prefer systems that are simple to reason about, even if they take more effort to build that way.

#### Career

I started programming in college and quickly found that backend systems and infrastructure were where I wanted to spend my time. Over the years I have worked on pricing engines, exchange services, product master systems, and assessment pipelines — each one teaching me something new about building things that hold up under real-world conditions.

#### Engineering Philosophy

- Simplicity is harder to achieve than complexity, and more valuable.
- Understanding the fundamentals pays compound interest over time.
- Operational concerns are not afterthoughts — they are part of the design.
- The best code is the code you do not have to write.

#### Leisure

Outside of engineering, I read non-fiction — mostly on history, philosophy, and technology. I enjoy poetry, chess, and cooking.
